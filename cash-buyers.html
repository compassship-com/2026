Green ship recycling refers to the environmentally responsible and sustainable dismantling of ships at the end of their operational life, ensuring minimal harm to the environment and adherence to international safety and labour standards. It focuses on reducing pollution, maximizing the recovery of reusable materials, and protecting workers' health and safety during the process. 

Key Aspects of Green Ship Recycling:

Environmentally Friendly Practices

> Proper handling and disposal of hazardous materials such as asbestos, polychlorinated biphenyls (PCBs), oils, and other toxic substances.

> Minimizing the release of pollutants into the soil, water, and air during dismantling operations.

Maximizing Material Recovery

> Recycling as much of the ship as possible, including steel, non-ferrous metals, machinery, and other reusable components.

Compliance with Regulations

> Aligning with global conventions such as: Hong Kong International Convention for the Safe and Environmentally Sound Recycling of Ships (2009).

> Adopting and arranging aligning with European Union Ship Recycling Regulation for EU-flagged ships.

Worker Safety

> Ensuring safe working conditions with proper training, protective equipment, and strict safety protocols to prevent accidents.

Benefits of Green Ship Recycling:

Environmental Protection: Reduces pollution and conserves natural resources.

Sustainability: Encourages the circular economy by reusing materials.

Regulatory Compliance: Helps ship-owners meet legal obligations and avoid penalties.

Corporate Responsibility: Enhances the reputation of companies by adopting eco-friendly practices.

Green ship recycling is essential for balancing the growing demand for ship dismantling with the need to protect the environment and human health.
